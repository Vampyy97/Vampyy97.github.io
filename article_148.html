Trump unveils 25% India levy

A clothing factory in Bhiwandi, outside Mumbai, yesterday as Donald Trump announced the US would slap 25 per cent tariffs on imports from India. The US president said New Delhi’s own levies were “among the highest in the world” but later said the two countries were still negotiating, adding “we'll see what happens.”

The planned levy will come with an additional, unspecified, penalty after Trump noted India had “always bought a majority of their military equipment from Russia” and was its biggest energy buyer despite the Ukraine war.

If implemented, the tariffs are likely to dent Apple’s plan to source 60mn iPhones sold each year in the US from India. New Delhi said it was studying the details but vowed to protect its interests.
