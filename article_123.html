Spotify ‘moving too slowly on advertising, says chief’

ANNA NICOLAOU — NEW YORK
DANIEL THOMAS — LONDON

Spotify swung to a net loss in the second quarter as the music streaming group had been “moving too slowly” on advertising, boss Daniel Ek said, sending shares down 11 per cent in New York late-morning trading yesterday.

The company reported a net loss of €86mn for the three months to the end of June, or €0.42 a share, down from a profit of €274mn in the same period last year — far below analysts’ expectations and following higher costs related to staff pay.

Ek said: “The one area that hasn't yet beaten expectations is our ads business ... We have simply been moving too slowly,” adding it was “really an execution challenge, not a problem with the strategy.”

Spotify, which has been introducing automated tools for companies to buy ads on its site, reported a 1 per cent fall in advertising revenues year on year.

The music streaming platform reported €4.2bn in revenue in the second quarter, up 10 per cent year on year but lower than its guidance, due to currency movements.

It said it was expecting operating income of €485mn in the third quarter and revenue of €4.2bn, both below expectations.

“As you look at the underlying fundamentals of the business, this year is largely playing out as expected,” Ek added.

The company’s shares have risen by 53 per cent in the year to date, following a cost-cutting push that led to its first-ever full year of profit in 2024.

Investors have been closely watching profitability after the group started to raise subscription prices, bring in new tiers and add to its services in audiobooks and podcasts.

Spotify in June raised prices in France, confirming an FT report that it was planning to increase prices in dozens of countries.

Subscribers climbed 12 per cent to 276mn, while monthly active users grew 11 per cent to 696mn.
