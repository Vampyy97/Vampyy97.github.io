Merck hit by drop in China sales of blockbuster vaccine

PATRICK TEMPLE-WEST — NEW YORK

Plunging sales of Merck’s blockbuster HPV vaccine in China have caused a sharp fall in the US pharmaceuticals group’s earnings, as the company announced job losses as part of a wider restructuring in a bid to cut costs.

In results yesterday, Merck reported that after the effect of currency fluctuations were stripped out, net income fell to $4.4bn in the second quarter, down 17 per cent year on year.

Merck had previously warned about the slump in sales of Gardasil vaccines — which are used to prevent cervical cancers and other conditions — saying in February that it was halting shipments to China to help a key local supplier reduce excess inventory.

Additionally, Merck said a restructuring was under way to save $3bn annually. This included an unspecified number of job cuts, though it would continue to hire. The company said it would also shrink its real estate holdings.

The Gardasil human papillomavirus virus vaccine remains Merck’s second best-selling drug behind cancer treatment Keytruda. Global Gardasil sales fell 55 per cent in the quarter to $1.1bn.

Merck said that stripping out China Gardasil figures, its total global sales would have increased 7 per cent.

Sales of Keytruda, the world’s best-selling drug, rose 7 per cent. The drug, whose patent expires in 2028, makes up more than half of Merck’s sales.

The drugmaker maintained its full-year sales guidance of $64.3bn-$65.3bn and affirmed a previous $200mn estimate of extra costs from new US tariffs.

Its earnings per share after currency effects were stripped out fell 16 per cent to $1.76, below analyst estimates. Shares were down more than 3 per cent in late-morning trading.
