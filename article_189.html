Alarm bells for UK savers

You report in an interview with pensions minister Torsten Bell (July 21) that the UK government is to create a commission to study further compulsory pension savings.

Anyone with a mortgage is already paying the financial services industry for the “privilege” of borrowing at rates that are usually higher than inflation. Extending compulsory saving will therefore cause such persons to also be borrowing to invest, and more people will become serfs, working for the financial services industry.

David Kauders Zug, Switzerland
