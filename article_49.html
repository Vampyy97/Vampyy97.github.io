Trump acknowledges ‘real starvation’ afflicting Gaza and promises more aid

FT Reporters

US President Donald Trump has said for the first time that “starvation” is afflicting people in Gaza as he pledged to get more food into the territory, saying he was working on “various plans” with the Israeli government.

Speaking with UK Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer as they held talks in Scotland yesterday, Trump said the images he had seen of malnourished children were real, despite claims from Israel’s Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu that there was no starvation in the strip.

“That’s real starvation stuff,” Trump said. “I see it, you can't fake that.”

“Those children look very hungry,” he added, describing what he'd seen on television. “We have to get the kids fed.”

The comments will increase pressure on Israel to expedite deliveries of aid into Gaza, as the UN and other bodies warn of the acute risk of famine. Gaza’s health ministry has reported more than 80 “famine and malnutrition-related deaths” in July, including 14 yesterday.

Starmer described the situation as “an absolute catastrophe” and said “people in Britain are revolted... at what they are seeing on their screens. So we have got to get to that ceasefire.”

Taking questions at Turnberry, one of Trump's Scottish golf courses, the president spoke primarily of short-term plans to get more food aid delivered but was vague on the details.

Downing Street had earlier said Starmer would present a “UK plan” for “sustainable peace” in the Middle East to Trump after discussing the proposal with German Chancellor Friedrich Merz and French President Emmanuel Macron at the weekend. London said Starmer and Trump had agreed “to work together to bring an end to the misery and starvation.”

Merz said Israel “must also do its part” to improve the situation, underlining how the staunch supporter of Israel has adopted a more critical stance towards Netanyahu’s government.

But the European plans appeared to have gained minimal traction with Trump, who was non-committal on what might happen in the next few days.

He said Washington would help “set up food centres where people can walk in with no boundaries,” in apparent criticism of the US-funded Gaza Humanitarian Foundation. Local officials say more than 1,000 people have been killed trying to reach aid in recent months, many in attacks by Israeli troops while trying to reach GHF sites.
