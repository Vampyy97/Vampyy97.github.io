Reeves rhetoric on planning bill is needlessly corrosive

It is deeply dismaying to read (“Reeves argues planning bill retreat will speed passage”, Report, July 23) that senior members of the UK government persist in waging a cynical culture war that vilifies endangered species such as bats as imaginary barriers to economic growth and infrastructure development.

In fact, the government's own impact assessment of the planning and infrastructure bill found no evidence to substantiate the tired trope that environmental protections are a blocker to development.

This month’s amendments to the planning bill were a welcome first step in ensuring it delivers for both nature and development. Yet such needless and harmful rhetoric from Rachel Reeves, the chancellor, further erodes the already fragile confidence in the government to uphold its recently restated commitment to safeguard our natural environment.

Sally Hayns
CEO, Chartered Institute of Ecology and Environmental Management, Romsey, Hampshire, UK
