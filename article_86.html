Insurers and their role in the next financial crisis

Pilita Clark’s insightful article on how the next financial crisis starts (Spectrum, Life & Arts, June 28) serves as a sobering reminder of the role insurance could play in a financial crisis caused by climate shocks.

It’s important to note, however, that our industry is not powerless in the face of a system meltdown. Insurers must work with policymakers, businesses and communities to move beyond the present focus on the short term. We have the insights and tools right now to avoid a catastrophic collapse. What’s missing is the will to use them.

Take flooding. The loss history at our company, FM, shows that facilities with well-organised flood emergency response plans experience nearly 70 per cent less damage than those without them.

Bringing these together with community-based plans can bring broader and more impactful resilience.

These and other fairly simple measures won’t reverse the general trend of increasing risk. They can, however, be done now as an initial step on the way to long-term resilience. And they are far superior to waiting for climate shocks to leave us with no choice.

Louis Gritzo
Chief Science Officer, FM (a commercial property insurer), Norwood, MA, US
