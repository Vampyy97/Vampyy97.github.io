The world is failing the Palestinian people

As starvation stalks Gaza, western allies must press for end to Israeli offensive

Rarely has the world had such visibility of mass human suffering as in Gaza. Images from the besieged Palestinian enclave are graphically displaying the death and destruction wrought by Israel’s nearly 22-month offensive. Women clutching horribly emaciated children. War-scarred hospitals struggling to treat streams of wounded. The dead lined up in rows in body bags. Mass starvation is stalking the strip.

Yet Israeli soldiers have killed hundreds of people trekking to aid distribution centres operated under a flawed Israeli- and US-backed system that breaks with usual humanitarian models. The number of Palestinians killed by Israel’s offensive is nearing 60,000, according to local health officials.

Across the strip, entire towns have been razed, with most of the 2.1mn population herded into barren wastelands. Israeli defence minister Israel Katz has told local media he wants to establish a “humanitarian city” in southern Gaza to house 600,000 people and later the entire population.

When Israel first launched its offensive, most of the world stood by a nation traumatised by the abhorrent October 7 2023 attack by Hamas, the Palestinian militant group, that killed 1,200 people. Other nations supported Israel’s right to self-defence, to hold the perpetrators to account and to attempt to secure the release of 250 hostages seized. Hamas should have released all hostages long ago. Its use of them as bargaining chips is despicable.

But Israel's offensive has long gone beyond all bounds of a proportionate response. The longer it has continued, the more it has borne the hallmarks of a vengeful assault by Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s far-right government to destroy the very fabric of Palestinian society.

The pattern is repeated, on a lesser scale, in the occupied West Bank. Israeli military operations and Jewish settler violence have forced thousands from their homes.

A small but increasing number of genocide scholars are concluding that Israel’s actions in Gaza amount to genocide. On Monday, B’Tselem, a respected Israeli rights group, reached the same verdict. In a report, it said Israel’s policy and “its horrific outcomes ... leads to the unequivocal conclusion that Israel is taking co-ordinated action to intentionally destroy Palestinian society in the Gaza Strip.”

Israel bitterly rejects the allegation. But evidence of ethnic cleansing is mounting, with the population forcibly displaced multiple times under the threat of military action. Aid is also indisputably being weaponised, and an entire community collectively punished for Hamas's appalling actions.

Yet Israel’s western allies still refuse to take the necessary steps to pressure Netanyahu to end its war — above all the Trump administration, which has the most leverage. Other western nations’ condemnations are not enough. Unless the Israeli government agrees to an immediate end to the war and a surge of aid into Gaza, western countries should be sanctioning Netanyahu and his government. They should halt all arms sales to Israel. More should follow French President Emmanuel Macron’s lead and recognise a Palestinian state.

Arab nations could also be doing more. Egypt, Jordan, the United Arab Emirates and others that have relations with Israel should make clear those diplomatic ties cannot be sustained without a radical change of course.

Israel this weekend announced “tactical pauses” and “humanitarian corridors”, in a sign that Netanyahu’s government is not immune to outside pressure. But it is far too little, far too late. The world is failing the Palestinian people — a failure that will haunt western nations in particular for years to come.
