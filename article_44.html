A bit of telephone etiquette I find useful to this day

Reading Pilita Clark’s Business Life column (“A fresh phone hell in the office”, July 21) reminded me of the training film I was shown when I started work in the 1970s.

Filmed I suspect many years previously, it assumed much of the audience would be unfamiliar with the telephone outside of the workplace.

It did however include one “hint” which I find useful to this day. When a call is unexpectedly disconnected the original instigator should be the one to redial. This avoids the problem as famously portrayed in the film 10 where Dudley Moore and Julie Andrews are at either end of the line, each trying to call but finding the other always engaged.
