Handbags help Hermès to rise above luxury malaise

Hermès’ sales rose 9 per cent in the second quarter, outpacing its rivals in a difficult luxury market as wealthy shoppers continued to shell out for its high-end bags.

The Paris-based luxury house yesterday reported sales of €3.9bn, a 9 per cent rise on the same period last year at constant exchange rates, and ahead of analyst forecasts. Operating income rose 6 per cent in the first half of the year to €3.3bn, beating expectations.

This year Hermès overtook LVMH to become the world’s most valuable luxury company by market capitalisation for the first time. But, despite relatively strong results, shares in Hermès fell 4.5 per cent. That trimmed their rise over the past year to 12 per cent, giving the company a market capitalisation of €243bn — just ahead of LVMH.

Bernstein analyst Luca Solca noted that weaker performance at some of Hermès’ smaller divisions was a sign the luxury sector remained under pressure.

Hermès said it would offset the impact of higher US tariffs by raising prices in the country. But executive chair Axel Dumas said he did not anticipate raising prices again after increasing them globally in May, including by an additional 5 per cent in the US.

Demand for Kelly and Birkin handbags drove organic growth of 12 per cent in Hermès’ leather goods division in the first half of the year. Sales in the fashion and silk divisions rose 6 and 4 per cent respectively, though revenues in its watchmaking unit fell in the period.

Hermès reported sales in the Americas rose 12.3 per cent in the second quarter, well ahead of analyst expectations, despite the price rise in the spring. Sales in Asia-Pacific, excluding Japan, rose 3 per cent despite a slowdown in Chinese spending on luxury more broadly.
